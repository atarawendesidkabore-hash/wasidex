## Investment Objective

Track the evolution of the main raw materials exported from Ghana through a transparent, rules-based benchmark measured in GHS.

### Economic Thesis

- Ghana is the world's second-largest cocoa producer and a major gold exporter, creating a diversified commodity export base.
- The Takoradi-Tema port corridor anchors a significant volume of mineral and agricultural export flows.
- A trailing 20-year tonnage-weighted benchmark can represent the physical structure of Ghana's export economy.
- GHS denomination makes the benchmark locally relevant for Ghanaian institutional investors.
- Ghana serves as a major corridor gateway for landlocked Burkina Faso and northern trade routes.

## Benchmark Methodology

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Weighting | Trailing 20-year average export tonnage |
| Reconstitution | Annual |
| Rebalancing | Quarterly |
| Pricing | Reference prices in GHS |
| Coverage | All major raw material exports |

**Caution:** Pure tonnage weighting materially reduces the weight of high-value low-mass exports such as gold.

## Corridor Relevance

Ghana is the primary corridor outlet for landlocked Burkina Faso. The Tema-Ouagadougou highway and rail links are critical trade arteries for West African commerce.
